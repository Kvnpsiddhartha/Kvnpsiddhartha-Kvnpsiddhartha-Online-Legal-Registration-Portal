Benefits of NGO Registration

Benefits of Tax Exemption

Registration of the company as an NGO under the Companies Act 2013 allows the company to enjoy several taxation benefits, e.g. Companies can save money by registering NGOs under the Companies Act, 2013

Right To Acquire Assets

Once your organisation has been officially registered, you can acquire land, own fixed assets, and acquire liability under the common seal. Unregistered organizations cannot buy, hold or sell land.

No Capital Requirement

To be able to operate independently, there is no minimum capital requirement. The charity can provide direct funding to NGOs. This allows NGOs to operate independently without a larger share of capital.

Protection from Personal Liabilit

You can purchase, acquire and register assets and stakes under the NGO's name. This is a well-organized way to protect yourself from untoward events, such as divorce, foreclosure, bankruptcy or judgement debt.

Corporate Entity

Your organization's relationships with the community and transactions will be improved as a corporate body. An NGO can sue to enforce its legal rights or be sued by its registered trustees.

Stamp Duty Exemption

Section 8 companies are exempted under the Income Tax Act from stamp duty. This allows for more tax-saving options for the company. Stamp duty saves taxes that can be used to promote the company's objectives. It also protects company funds, which helps improve the company's efficiency and productivity.
